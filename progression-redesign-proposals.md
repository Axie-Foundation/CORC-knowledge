# Axie Classic -- Progression Redesign Proposals

**Date:** 2026-05-11
**Goal:** Replace vertical progression (higher stats = win more) with horizontal progression (more options = play differently). Create a level playing field where match outcomes are determined by player skill and decisions, not Axie level or unlocked parts.

## The Core Problem Being Solved

This is a fundamental design shift -- moving from vertical progression (higher stats = win more) to horizontal progression (more options = play differently).

The current system creates a progression treadmill that determines match outcomes: higher-level Axies with more unlocked parts beat lower-level ones regardless of skill. This means:

- New players lose to veterans because of stats, not decisions
- The "game" is grinding levels, not playing well
- Matchmaking can't create fair matches across progression tiers

The goal is progression that feels rewarding without creating power gaps. Players should feel like they're getting stronger at the *game*, not just making their numbers bigger.

**Design Principles:**
1. No progression system should create a power gap between players
2. Rewards should be cosmetic, knowledge-based, or social (not stat increases)
3. Players should earn dopamine hits from skill expression and exploration, not grinding
4. Small-scale financial incentives are acceptable but must not affect gameplay
5. The system should encourage breadth (try new things) over depth (optimize one thing)

## Proposal 1: Mastery Track System (Per-Axie Skill Trees)

Each Axie has a Mastery Track -- a progression path unlocked by playing that specific Axie in battles. Instead of raw stat increases, mastery unlocks cosmetic variations, alternate card effects, and playstyle modifiers that are sidegrades, not upgrades.

### How It Works
- Every battle played with an Axie earns Mastery XP for that Axie
- Mastery has 50 levels per Axie, with milestone rewards every 5 levels
- Rewards include: alternate card art, emote unlocks, card effect variants (same power budget, different utility), title badges ("Beast Master Rank 3")
- At Mastery 25 and 50, unlock a Signature Card Variant -- a reskinned version of one of the Axie's cards with identical stats but a different visual effect and flavor text

### Dopamine Hits
- XP bar fills after every match (win or lose, winners get more)
- Milestone popups every 5 levels with a reward chest animation
- Mastery borders on your Axie portrait that opponents can see
- Leaderboard: "Most Mastered Axies" -- shows dedication, not spending

### Why It Doesn't Create Power Gaps
- Zero stat increases from mastery
- Card variants are cosmetic or strict sidegrades (e.g., same damage but Bleed instead of Poison -- different, not better)
- A mastery-1 Axie and a mastery-50 Axie are equally powerful in combat

### Financial Incentive
Mastery 50 unlocks the ability to mint the Signature Card Variant as a tradeable cosmetic NFT. Small, optional, doesn't affect gameplay.

## Proposal 2: Seasonal Challenge Passport

Replace the current battle pass with a Challenge Passport -- a seasonal booklet of specific combat achievements that reward completion, not grinding. Each season introduces new challenges that push players to try different strategies.

### How It Works
- Each season has 100 challenges organized into themed chapters (e.g., "Beast Unleashed," "The Tank Line," "Speed Kills")
- Challenges are specific: "Win a game using 3 Beast cards in a single combo," "Win with all 3 Axies alive," "Deal 500+ damage in a single round," "Win a game without using energy in round 1"
- Completing challenges earns Passport Stamps that fill a collection page
- Completing a full chapter unlocks a themed cosmetic set (card backs, arena skins, profile frames)
- Completing 80/100 challenges unlocks the Season Title (e.g., "Strategist of Season 7")

### Dopamine Hits
- Challenge completion notification mid-match ("Challenge Complete: First Blood!")
- Collection page with visual stamp-collecting satisfaction
- Chapter completion unlocks with reveal animation
- End-of-season showcase comparing your passport with friends
- "Personal Best" tracking for challenge speed-running

### Why It Doesn't Create Power Gaps
- Rewards are purely cosmetic
- Challenges test skill and creativity, not level or stats
- A new player can complete challenges as easily as a veteran if they play well

### Financial Incentive
Premium passport ($5) adds 25 bonus challenges with exclusive cosmetic rewards. No gameplay advantage.

## Proposal 3: Axie Training Grounds (Roguelike PvE Mode)

A single-player/co-op mode where players take their Axies through procedurally generated encounters, earning temporary in-run buffs and permanent cosmetic/collection rewards. Think Slay the Spire meets Axie.

### How It Works
- Enter with your team of 3 Axies
- Progress through 12 floors of increasingly difficult AI opponents
- Between floors, choose from 3 random Training Modifiers (e.g., "+20% crit chance this run," "All Beast cards cost 0 energy this run," "Your Axies heal 10 HP per round this run")
- Modifiers stack -- by floor 12, your team is absurdly powerful (but so are the enemies)
- Completing a run earns Training Tokens based on floors cleared
- Training Tokens buy cosmetics from a rotating shop: alternate Axie poses, victory animations, card effect colors

### Dopamine Hits
- Roguelike "broken build" moments when modifiers stack well
- Floor-clear rewards with increasing rarity
- Weekly leaderboard: deepest floor reached / fastest clear
- Daily rotating "Featured Challenge" (e.g., "Clear floor 8 using only 0-cost cards") with bonus tokens

### Why It Doesn't Create Power Gaps
- All modifiers are temporary (in-run only)
- Training Tokens buy cosmetics, not power
- PvP is completely unaffected by Training Grounds progression
- Provides a fun way to "practice" with your Axies without ranked stakes

### Financial Incentive
Premium Training Grounds pass unlocks a harder "Nightmare" difficulty with exclusive cosmetic rewards. Optional co-op mode where 2 players combine teams (6 Axies) against harder content.

## Proposal 4: Strategist Rank System (Skill-Based Ladder with Role Rewards)

Replace the current ranked system with a Strategist Rank that measures decision-making quality, not just win/loss, and rewards players for developing expertise with specific team archetypes.

### How It Works
- After each match, both players receive a Strategy Score (0-100) based on:
  - Card efficiency (damage dealt per energy spent)
  - Prediction accuracy (did your shields block damage? did you target correctly?)
  - Combo execution (did you maximize skill bonus?)
  - Adaptability (did you change strategy when behind?)
- Strategy Score contributes to your Strategist Rank (Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum, Diamond, Master)
- Separate Archetype Badges track your experience with team compositions (e.g., "Beast Rush" badge, "Turtle Defense" badge, "Poison Control" badge)
- Each Archetype Badge has 3 tiers (Bronze/Silver/Gold) unlocked by playing that archetype well (not just often)

### Dopamine Hits
- Post-match Strategy Score breakdown (like a report card)
- Rank-up animations with league-specific arena cosmetics
- Archetype Badge unlocks with themed card backs
- Monthly "Strategist Spotlight" featuring top-scoring players (not just highest rank)
- "Personal Best" notifications when you beat your highest Strategy Score

### Why It Doesn't Create Power Gaps
- Strategy Score is normalized for team composition -- a Beast Rush team is scored against Beast Rush benchmarks
- Rank determines matchmaking, not power level
- All players at all levels can earn high Strategy Scores
- Archetype Badges encourage trying different teams rather than optimizing one

### Financial Incentive
End-of-season rewards scale with Strategist Rank -- small SLP/AXS amounts at Diamond+, cosmetics at all ranks. Top 100 Strategists get a unique seasonal title.

## Proposal 5: Collection Atlas (Horizontal Breadth Progression)

Instead of deepening one team, reward players for broadening their collection and knowledge. The Collection Atlas is a visual map of all Axie classes, card combinations, and team archetypes that players "explore" by playing.

### How It Works
- The Atlas is a visual hex-grid map organized by class combinations
- Each hex represents a team archetype (e.g., "Beast/Plant/Aquatic," "Triple Bug," "Dawn/Dusk/Mech")
- Playing a match with a team archetype explores that hex, filling in its art and lore
- Each hex has 3 exploration tiers:
  - Discovered (play 1 match): reveals the hex art
  - Studied (play 10 matches): unlocks a lore entry about that archetype's strategy
  - Mastered (win 25 matches with a high Strategy Score): unlocks a themed emote and card back
- Adjacent hexes form regions -- completing all hexes in a region unlocks a region reward (profile frame, arena theme)
- The full Atlas has ~50 hexes covering all viable team compositions

### Dopamine Hits
- Map exploration -- "fog of war" lifts as you try new teams
- Lore unlocks tell the story of Lunacia through gameplay
- Region completion is a major milestone with fanfare
- Atlas completion % displayed on profile (bragging rights)
- Weekly "Expedition" quest: "Explore a hex you haven't visited" with bonus rewards

### Why It Doesn't Create Power Gaps
- All rewards are cosmetic/knowledge
- Encourages trying many different teams rather than grinding one
- New players and veterans have equal access -- the Atlas resets each major season with new hexes
- Creates a natural incentive to own diverse Axies (breadth over depth)

### Financial Incentive
"Rare Expeditions" are limited-time hexes (e.g., "Triple Mech" during a Mech-themed event) with exclusive cosmetics. Completing them all during the event window earns a commemorative collectible.

## Comparison Matrix

| System | Progression Type | Primary Reward | Grind Feel | Levels Playing Field? |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|------------|----------------------|
| 1. Mastery Track | Per-Axie depth | Card variants, cosmetics | Low-medium (natural play) | Yes (no stat gains) |
| 2. Challenge Passport | Seasonal achievements | Cosmetic sets, titles | Low (skill-based) | Yes (skill-gated, not level-gated) |
| 3. Training Grounds | PvE roguelike | Cosmetics, practice | Medium (fun grind) | Yes (PvE-only power, no PvP impact) |
| 4. Strategist Rank | Skill measurement | Rank cosmetics, small financial | Low (quality over quantity) | Yes (measures decisions, not stats) |
| 5. Collection Atlas | Breadth exploration | Lore, cosmetics, profile | Medium (encourages variety) | Yes (rewards trying new things) |

---

## Recommended Combination

Systems 2 + 4 + 5 layer naturally together:

- **Challenge Passport** gives short-term goals (complete this challenge today)
- **Strategist Rank** gives competitive identity (I'm a Diamond-tier strategist)
- **Collection Atlas** gives long-term exploration (I've mastered 35 of 50 archetypes)

Together they create a progression loop that rewards skill, creativity, and breadth -- not grinding stats. Each system feeds into the others: exploring new Atlas hexes naturally completes Passport challenges, which improves your Strategy Score across archetypes.

**System 1 (Mastery Track)** adds per-Axie attachment if the team wants players to bond with specific Axies. **System 3 (Training Grounds)** adds a PvE escape valve for players who want progression without competitive pressure.

All 5 can coexist, but the recommended core is 2 + 4 + 5.
