# Ten Ways to Spend Gems — Plain-Language Summary

## Why this matters now
Today, players mainly spend gems on gear crafting and gear upgrades. Both of those are going away with the artifact/gear removal. At the same time, the new chest, Battle Pass, and compensation systems are all going to **add** gems to the economy. If we don't add new things to spend them on, gems will lose their value fast — and players stop caring about a currency that buys nothing.

The ten ideas below are designed to give players a wide menu of reasons to spend gems, hitting different motivations: showing off, getting ahead, taking another shot at something, helping their guild, and so on.

## The ten

### 1. Cosmetic Axie skins
Visual themes for Axies — alternate looks that don't change stats. Common skin small price, legendary skin big price. Players spend, then show off every match.

**Why players want it:** Status and identity. The Axie is the player's signature — letting them customise it visually is a strong, repeat-purchase sink.

---

### 2. Battle Pass premium unlock (keep what we have)
The Battle Pass premium track unlock is already in the game and already costs gems. Keep this — it's a once-a-season major purchase that absorbs a big spike of gems.

**Why players want it:** It unlocks the entire premium reward track for the season. "I'm in" decisions are some of the highest-converting purchases in any game.

---

### 3. Battle Pass tier skip
Spend gems to instantly buy a tier or two of Battle Pass progress. Helps late starters catch up, and helps engaged players grab that last reward at season end.

**Why players want it:** Removes the anxiety of "I'll never finish in time." Late-season FOMO is a strong driver.

---

### 4. Extra daily wheel spins
The daily wheel gives free spins. After the free ones are used, let players buy more for gems — with a daily cap and rising cost per extra spin.

**Why players want it:** The wheel's random reward is the strongest dopamine trigger we have. "Just one more spin" is a real human urge.

---

### 5. Extra Cursed Coliseum sessions
Today, players are capped at 2 Coliseum sessions per day. Let them spend gems for a 3rd session, with a weekly cap of one or two paid sessions.

**Why players want it:** Engaged players on a hot streak hate hitting the daily cap. We sell them one more shot exactly when they want it most.

---

### 6. Buy lottery tickets directly
If we keep the lottery feature, let players buy tickets with gems instead of waiting for drops.

**Why players want it:** Jackpot-style anticipation. This is the most direct gambling-style sink we have. **Note:** depends on the lottery feature staying — if not, replace with a daily limited-time bundle in the shop instead.

---

### 7. Paid tournament entry tiers
Add a paid bracket on top of the existing tournament system. Bigger entry fee, bigger prize pool.

**Why players want it:** Competitive players self-select into a bigger-stakes bracket. The win feels bigger because the prize is bigger.

---

### 8. Quest reroll and skip
Don't like the quest you got? Reroll it for a small gem fee. Don't have time to grind a quest you want the reward for? Skip it for a bigger gem fee.

**Why players want it:** Removes friction from a daily activity. Reroll is cheap and frequent; skip is expensive and deliberate.

---

### 9. Guild treasury donations and group perks
Let players donate gems to a guild treasury. Guild leadership can then spend the pooled gems on guild-wide perks — for example, +5% gold drops for everyone in the guild for a week, or a guild banner that shows on every member's profile.

**Why players want it:** Social. Top donators become recognised inside the guild. The whole guild gets the benefit, so spending feels generous, not just self-indulgent.

---

### 10. Tower of Solo retry and floor skip
Failed a tough Tower floor? Pay gems to retry it without losing your overall progress. Already cleared a floor before? Pay gems to skip the early-floor warm-up and jump straight to where you left off.

**Why players want it:** "I almost had it!" is one of the strongest comeback hooks in games. And veteran players hate redoing easy content — letting them skip is pure quality of life.

---

## Phase 2 candidates (if any of the top ten has problems)
- **Profile frames, banners, name colours, titles** — persistent status, easy to keep adding new ones.
- **Card cosmetics** — alternate card art and card backs. Every battle shows them.
- **Daily rotating shop deals** — a 24-hour limited offer that creates FOMO and builds the daily-login habit.
- **Per-Axie experience boosters** — speed up the AXP progression for a specific Axie.
- **Axie or team renaming** — a small, repeat vanity purchase.

## How to keep gems valuable

The hardest part of this isn't picking sinks — it's making sure the sinks together absorb gems faster than the new faucets create them.

**The right portfolio looks like this:**

- **Daily small purchases** (wheel spins, quest rerolls — 10 to 50 gems) — the constant drip
- **Weekly medium purchases** (extra Coliseum session, lottery bundle, Tower retry — 50 to 500 gems) — the regular spend
- **Monthly / seasonal larger purchases** (skins, BP tier skips, tournament entries — 500 to 2000 gems) — the meaningful decisions
- **Per-season major purchases** (BP premium unlock, epic skins, guild perks — 2000+ gems) — the big commitments

A casual player should engage with 1–3 sinks. A regular player with 5–7. A heavy spender with all ten and the seasonal premium tier.

**Three rules to keep gems valuable:**

1. **Audit the gem flow every quarter.** Measure how many gems players are earning vs. how many they're spending. If they're earning faster than they spend, prices need to go up or rewards need to go down.
2. **Don't pay gems back from gem-paid features.** If the Battle Pass premium track (which players bought with gems) gives them back more gems than they paid, the feature becomes a money printer instead of a sink.
3. **Be transparent about pricing tiers.** Players accept a 5000-gem epic skin if they understand "that's the legendary tier." They resent it if the pricing feels arbitrary.

## Recommended order to ship them

**With the artifact/gear removal release:** wheel refills, quest reroll/skip — these are the cheapest to build and absorb the most daily gems immediately.

**Within a month:** Axie skins (start with a small launch catalogue), Battle Pass tier skip, Coliseum extra session.

**Within two months:** Paid tournament brackets, guild treasury perks, Tower retry/skip.

**When scoped:** Lottery ticket purchase (or the daily limited-time bundle alternative if lottery is being deprecated).

## Questions to settle before this can ship
1. Is the lottery feature staying after the artifact removal? If not, swap idea 6 for "daily rotating shop deals."
2. Do we want gems to feel **generous** (plentiful, premium tier rare) or **tight** (gems feel valuable, premium tier accessible only to heavy spenders)?
3. Should guild perks give real gameplay advantages, or stay cosmetic-only (safer for competitive balance)?
4. Are paid Axie skins fine to monetize aggressively, or should there be a free path to earn them too?
5. How comfortable are we with limited-edition / time-limited items? FOMO sells, but it can also burn out players if overused.
